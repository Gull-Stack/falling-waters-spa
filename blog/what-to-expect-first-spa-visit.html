What to Expect at Your First Spa Visit

By Falling Waters Day Spa | Updated February 2026 | 6 min read

Walking into a day spa for the first time can feel a little intimidating. What do you wear? Where do you go? What if you do something wrong? Take a deep breath â€” we've got you covered.

At Falling Waters Day Spa in Draper, Utah, we welcome first-time spa-goers every day. This guide will walk you through everything you need to know so you can relax and enjoy your experience from the moment you arrive.

Before You Arrive

Booking Your Appointment

When booking, mention that it's your first spa visit. Our staff can:

  • Recommend the best services for beginners
  • Allow extra time for consultation
  • Answer any questions you have in advance

Arrive Early

Plan to arrive 10-15 minutes before your appointment. This gives you time to:

  • Complete any intake forms
  • Use the restroom
  • Start relaxing before your treatment
  • Discuss any concerns with your therapist

Avoid caffeine for a few hours before your appointment â€” it can make it harder to relax and may increase sensitivity during treatments.

What to Wear (and What Not to Worry About)

This is the #1 question we get from first-time guests. Here's the simple answer:

Getting There

Wear comfortable, casual clothes. You'll change into a robe once you arrive, so there's no need to dress up.

During Your Treatment

You'll undress to your comfort level. For massages:

  • Most people undress completely or wear underwear
  • You'll be professionally draped with sheets at all times
  • Only the area being worked on is exposed
  • Your comfort is the priority â€” undress only as much as you're comfortable with

What Happens When You Arrive

  1. Check-in: Our front desk will greet you and have you complete a brief health questionnaire
  2. Consultation: Your therapist or esthetician will discuss your goals and any concerns
  3. Change: You'll be shown to a changing area and given a robe and slippers
  4. Relax: Wait in our relaxation area until your therapist comes to get you
  5. Treatment: Enjoy your service â€” communicate throughout about pressure and comfort
  6. Aftercare: Take your time getting dressed; enjoy tea and the relaxation area

Spa Etiquette: The Basics

  • Silence your phone (or leave it in your locker)
  • Speak softly in common areas
  • Shower before using pools or saunas
  • Arrive on time â€” late arrivals may shorten your treatment
  • Communicate with your therapist about pressure and comfort
  • Tipping is customary (15-20% is standard)

Should You Talk During Your Treatment?

It's completely up to you! Some people prefer silence to fully relax, while others like to chat. Your therapist will follow your lead. If you want quiet time, it's perfectly okay to close your eyes and drift off.

Common First-Time Concerns

"What if I fall asleep?"

This happens all the time and is actually a compliment to your therapist! It means you're truly relaxed. We'll gently wake you when your treatment ends.

"What if I make a noise?"

Stomach gurgles, sighs, and even light snoring are completely normal. Your therapist has heard it all and won't think twice about it.

"What if I'm ticklish?"

Let your therapist know! They can adjust their technique to use firmer pressure, which typically feels less ticklish.
